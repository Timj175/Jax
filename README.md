## Housing & Neighborhood

### Popular Jacksonville Neighborhoods

#### Riverside/Avondale
- Historic neighborhood with tree-lined streets
- Walkable with shops, restaurants, bars
- Dog-friendly with parks nearby
- Rent: $$$ (Higher end)
- Great for young professionals

#### San Marco
- Charming neighborhood with boutique shops
- Dog-friendly with San Marco Square
- Walkable, community-oriented
- Rent: $$$ (Higher end)
- Family-friendly

#### Murray Hill
- Hip, up-and-coming neighborhood
- More affordable than Riverside
- Local breweries and eateries
- Rent: $$ (Moderate)
- Great for young adults

#### Beaches (Jacksonville Beach, Neptune Beach, Atlantic Beach)
- Beach lifestyle, outdoor activities
- Very dog-friendly
- Active social scene
- Rent: $$-$$$ (Varies by proximity to beach)
- Great for active lifestyles

#### Springfield
- Historic district under revitalization
- More affordable options
- Growing arts scene
- Rent: $ - $$ (Affordable to moderate)
- Up-and-coming area

#### Southside/Tinseltown
- Suburban feel, family-friendly
- More space, newer developments
- Car-dependent
- Rent: $$ (Moderate)
- Good schools

## Dog Information

### Pre-Move Preparation
- [ ] Schedule vet appointment for health certificate (required for interstate travel)
- [ ] Update vaccinations (especially rabies - required in FL)
- [ ] Get copies of all medical records
- [ ] Microchip dog if not already done
- [ ] Purchase travel crate or harness for car safety
- [ ] Pack dog's essentials (food, bowls, favorite toys, leash, waste bags)
- [ ] Research pet-friendly hotels if overnight stay needed

### Florida Requirements
- **Rabies Vaccination:** Required for all dogs (must be current)
- **Dog License:** Required in most Florida counties
  - Duval County: $15-25 annually (cheaper if spayed/neutered)
  - Must show proof of rabies vaccination
- **Leash Laws:** Dogs must be on leash in public areas
- **No Breed Restrictions:** Florida has no statewide breed bans (check apartment policies)

### Finding a New Veterinarian
**Recommended Vet Clinics in Jacksonville:**
- First Coast No More Homeless Pets (affordable)
- Beaches Animal Hospital
- San Marco Animal Hospital
- Southside Animal Hospital & Pet Resort

**Questions to Ask:**
- [ ] Are they accepting new patients?
- [ ] Do they have emergency services?
- [ ] What are their hours?
- [ ] Cost of routine care?
- [ ] Do they accept pet insurance?

### Dog Parks & Pet-Friendly Areas

#### Off-Leash Dog Parks
- **Ed Austin Regional Park Dog Park** (Southside) - 40+ acres, separate small/large dog areas
- **Dogwood Park** (Mandarin) - 21 acres, wooded trails, lake access
- **Riverside Dog Park** (Riverside) - Urban park, fenced, water fountains
- **Fort Caroline Dog Park** (Arlington) - Waterfront, shade trees
- **Atlantic Beach Dog Park** (Beaches) - Near the ocean, shaded

#### Pet-Friendly Activities
- Most Jacksonville beaches allow dogs (leashed)
- Many breweries and restaurants with outdoor seating welcome dogs
- Riverside Arts Market (Saturdays) - dog-friendly
- Numerous walking trails: Riverwalk, Timucuan Trail, etc.

#### Pet Services
- **Grooming:** PetSmart, Petco, local groomers
- **Boarding/Daycare:** Camp Bow Wow, K9 Safari, Woofs & Wags
- **Pet Stores:** Pet Supermarket, Hollywood Feed, Mud Bay
- **Emergency Vet:** BluePearl Pet Hospital (24/7), AVETS (24/7)

### Travel Tips for Dogs
- Stop every 2-3 hours for bathroom breaks
- Keep dog hydrated
- Don't leave dog in hot car (FL heat!)
- Bring familiar items (blanket, toys) for comfort
- Consider calming aids if dog has travel anxiety
- Update ID tags with new Jacksonville contact info

## Utilities & Services Setup

### Essential Utilities

#### Electric
- **Provider:** JEA (Jacksonville Electric Authority)
- **Phone:** 904-665-6000
- **Website:** jea.com
- **Setup:** Schedule 2-3 days before move-in
- **Deposit:** May be required, waived with good credit
- **Budget:** $100-200/month (varies by usage and season)

#### Water/Sewer/Trash
- **Provider:** JEA (same as electric)
- **Included in JEA service**
- **Budget:** $50-80/month

#### Internet/Cable/Phone
**Options:**
- **Xfinity/Comcast** - Cable/Internet bundle
- **AT&T Fiber** - High-speed internet (if available)
- **Spectrum** - Cable/Internet
- **T-Mobile/Verizon Home Internet** - Wireless option

**Recommendations:**
- Schedule installation 1 week before or day after move-in
- Compare bundle deals
- Check for new customer promotions
- Budget: $50-150/month

#### Gas
- **Provider:** TECO Peoples Gas
- **Only if property has gas appliances**
- **Budget:** $30-80/month (if applicable)

### Other Important Services

#### Renters/Home Insurance
- Required by most landlords
- Protects personal belongings
- Liability coverage
- Shop around: Lemonade, State Farm, Allstate, USAA
- Budget: $15-50/month

#### Mail Forwarding
- **USPS Change of Address:** usps.com/move
- **Cost:** $1.10 verification fee

## Notes & Reminders

### Florida-Specific Considerations
- **Hurricane Season:** June 1 - November 30
  - Purchase hurricane supplies (water, batteries, flashlights)
  - Know evacuation routes
  - Consider renter's insurance with hurricane coverage
- **Heat & Humidity:** Very hot and humid summers (prepare for AC costs)
- **No State Income Tax:** One of the benefits of FL!
- **Homestead Exemption:** If buying property, file for tax benefits
- **Insurance:** Auto insurance may be higher in FL

### Jacksonville Tips
- Download JEA app for utility management
- Get a SunPass for tolls
- Explore different neighborhoods before committing
- Join neighborhood Facebook groups
- Take advantage of the beaches and outdoor activities
- "Bold City" is Jacksonville's nickname
- St. Johns River runs north (one of few rivers in US)
